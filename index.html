Brand Snapshot
Background

Flair Airlines is Canada's sole independent ultra-low-cost carrier. 20 Boeing 737s, 28 destinations across Canada, the US, Mexico, and the Caribbean, and 4.5 million passengers in 2024. Headquartered in Edmonton, with operations in Toronto and Calgary. Now repositioning as a "value carrier" under new CEO Len Corrado (Feb 2026).

The turnaround story is real: 99% completion factor and best on-time performance in Canada in 2025. Recent route launches to Montego Bay, Jamaica and resumed Toronto-Montreal service signal continued network growth, but very little on social media tells that story. These are exactly the kind of moments that should be content events. Instead, they pass quietly.

The perception gap is equally real. Legacy reputation damage from 2022-23 (mass cancellations, baggage controversies) still defines how many Canadians think about Flair. Social media is the fastest channel to close that gap. Right now, it isn't being used that way.

Key Findings
Summary
📊
Content is transactional, not relational
The feed is dominated by fare sales, product explainers, and baggage education. This content is useful — the baggage tips TikTok is Flair's best performer — but it doesn't build the kind of audience that shares, follows, and advocates. Across 20 airlines analyzed, personality-driven content outperforms transactional content by 3–10x on engagement.
Both Platforms · Content Strategy
🧑‍✈️
Crew and people content is almost nonexistent
Crew-led content is the single most consistent performer across all 25 airlines in this competitive analysis. Virgin Atlantic, EasyJet, Southwest, Etihad — every airline that breaks out on TikTok leads with people. Flair has a young crew, an energetic brand, and accessible routes. That story isn't being told.
Both Platforms · Brand Story
▶
TikTok is underperforming the competitive set
Flair ranks last among Canada's Big 4 airlines on TikTok: Air Canada (129K), Porter (52K), WestJet (28K), Flair (22.9K). More importantly, Flair's content isn't producing outliers. Across this dataset, airlines with similar or smaller followings (Icelandair at 4.4K, JetBlue at 70K) produce viral content at 10–170x their median. Flair doesn't.
TikTok · Growth
💬
Negative UGC is dominating the conversation
A significant volume of organic TikTok content about Flair is negative — complaints about baggage fees, cancellations, and hidden costs. When the brand's own content doesn't generate enough positive signal, negative UGC fills the vacuum. The best defense is a strong content offense.

Signature Series

Bingeable, repeatable formats that build cult followings. The kind of content people come back for, subscribe for, and tell their friends about. The through-line on all of these: Flair is the enabler, not the subject. The series works because the people are the content. You're just the reason they showed up.

"Crew Unfiltered" — Reality Show
This is what gets virality. It's unique, it creates something people will want to binge, and no other airline is doing it. This is what creates mini internet celebs. This isn't about flights. It's about 18-hour layovers, airport chaos, random city decisions, personalities clashing, and inside jokes forming. Flair just happens to be the reason they're there.
The Shift
Before: "What crew do on layovers"
After: "Follow THIS crew member and what happens to them"

You're not filming content. You're following people.
The Cast
The Planner: "We need a schedule, guys"
The Chaos One: "Let's just see what happens"
The Local Expert: "Trust me, I know a spot"
The Tired One: "I'm not leaving the hotel"
The Main Character: Camera just naturally follows them

You don't script this. You cast it.
Episode Format
Hook: Something slightly chaotic or personal
Shot 1: Tight, mid-action. Someone already talking, walking, reacting
Context: "Layover: 18 hours" or "We land at 6pm. Flight's at 10am."
Build: Crew walking out of airport → split opinions (food first vs nap first) → city exploration → what they eat, where they go
Escalation: Someone gets lost. Someone regrets a decision. Something doesn't go as planned.
Final: Back at airport or unresolved moment
What Makes It Bingeable
Confessionals: "I knew this was a bad idea" / "I'm actually having the best time"
Running jokes: Same person always hungry. Same person always late. Same person always right.
Soft tension: "We should've listened to her" / "Why are we doing this again?"
The "Social Crew" becomes not people helping marketing — but the cast of the show.
Example Hooks
"We had 18 hours and made one terrible decision" "This is why you don't let him plan anything" "We said we'd take it easy… we did not" "I almost didn't leave the hotel and I regret everything"
"Who Are You Flying For?"
Candid airport interviews. One question, different answers every time. Grandma's birthday. A job interview. Seeing a best friend for the first time in two years. The flight is never the story. The person always is. Flair just showed up.
"First Time"
Document first flights. Kids. Adults who've never flown. Elderly passengers hitting a bucket list destination. No scripting. Just real reactions at the gate, on the plane, at arrival. This is high emotion, low cost, and repeatable forever.
"Mini Pilot"
A kid making the first announcement at the gate for boarding. Different kid every time. Hand them the mic, let them call the flight, film the cabin's reaction. Heartwarming, shareable, and infinitely repeatable.
"The Goodbye"
Arrivals and departures are one of the most emotionally loaded places on earth and almost no brand is actually sitting there with a camera. Short, quiet, real. No commentary needed. Let the hugs do the work.
"Ask a Local"
Crew from different cities give their honest, unglamorous recommendations. Not "visit the Old Port." More like "there's a 24-hour pho spot near the airport and it changes your life." Specific, real, searchable.
"Trip Fits"
Passengers doing a runway walk on the moving walkways. What they're wearing, where they're headed. Fashion meets travel, and the airport is the runway. Slow-mo optional but encouraged.
"Made It or Missed It"
This is your engine.
Real people trying to catch their flight. Running through the airport. Last call. Gate closing. We don't know if they make it.

Methodology
How this analysis was built — data collection, normalization, and outlier detection
Data Collection

TikTok posts scraped via Apify for 25 airline accounts. Each dataset contains all available posts with full metadata: play counts, engagement metrics (likes, shares, saves, comments), video duration, hashtags, and timestamps.

Normalization

Raw view counts favor large accounts. We calculate views per follower to level the playing field. A 1M-view post from a 50K-follower account (20x) signals stronger content than 5M views from a 3.5M-follower account (1.4x).

Outlier Detection

For each airline, we calculate the median views-per-follower ratio. Posts scoring >2x the median OR >1.5 standard deviations above the mean are flagged. Top 5 per airline = 125 outliers total.

Strategic Tagging

Each outlier tagged for content theme (10 categories), repeatability (repeatable / moment-dependent / brand-specific), and Flair relevance (steal / adapt / ignore).
